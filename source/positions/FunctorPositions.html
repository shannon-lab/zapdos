FunctorPositions

Computes positions from groups of three functors specified by the user

See the Functor for the list of available functors. The functors are evaluated at the origin of the mesh at the current time.

Example File Syntax

In this example, the FunctorPositions is obtaining the positions from three time-dependent functions. The functions are evaluated at the origin of the mesh at the current time.

[Positions<<<{"href": "../../syntax/Positions/index.html"}>>>]
  [functors]
    type = FunctorPositions<<<{"description": "Computes positions from groups of three functors specified by the user", "href": "FunctorPositions.html"}>>>
    positions_functors<<<{"description": "Functors providing the XYZ coordinates of the positions. A functor is any of the following: a variable, a functor material property, a function, a postprocessor or a number."}>>> = '0.1 0 0.3
                          f1 f2 f1'
  []
[]
(moose/test/tests/positions/functor_positions.i)

Input Parameters

  • positions_functorsFunctors providing the XYZ coordinates of the positions. A functor is any of the following: a variable, a functor material property, a function, a postprocessor or a number.

    Controllable:No

    Description:Functors providing the XYZ coordinates of the positions. A functor is any of the following: a variable, a functor material property, a function, a postprocessor or a number.
